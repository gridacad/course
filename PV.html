Course Overview

This 5-Level professional course equips electrical engineering students and professionals with the theoretical foundation and practical skills to design, size, and evaluate photovoltaic systems across all application types.

From PV physics and solar radiation analysis to full grid-tied and off-grid system design, software simulation, and financial feasibility, this course delivers industry-aligned competencies you can use from day one on the job.

Each lecture blends theory, applied engineering calculations, and practical simulation using industry-standard software — progressing logically from fundamentals to real project execution in Egypt and the wider MENA region.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Understand PV physics, I–V characteristics, and solar radiation analysis.
  • Select and size PV modules, inverters, and BOS components using datasheets.
  • Design complete grid-tied PV systems manually and using simulation software.
  • Size batteries, charge controllers, and off-grid/hybrid system components.
  • Engineer solar pumping systems including pump selection and PV sizing.
  • Develop project financial models: ROI, LCOE, and feasibility analysis (Egypt focus).
  • Navigate permits, regulations, and real project development workflows.

Course Structure

A carefully sequenced curriculum: each level builds on the previous, moving from concept to calculation to real-world execution

Level 1: Foundations of Solar & PV

Build strong conceptual and physical understanding of solar energy and PV behavior

Lecture 1 (2.5h): Introduction to Solar Energy

Course overview, solar radiation basics, and an introduction to solar energy technologies — PV vs CSP vs Solar Thermal — and their applications across grid-tied, off-grid, and BIPV systems.

Lecture 2 (2.5h): Photovoltaic Effect & PV Cells

Physics of the PV effect, I–V curve fundamentals, key parameters (Voc, Isc, MPP), efficiency factors, and losses.

Lecture 3 (2.5h): PV Modules & Technologies

From cell to module to array — series/parallel effects, mono/poly/thin-film technologies, datasheets, certification standards, and energy payback.

Lecture 4 (2.5h): Solar Radiation & Site Analysis

Radiation data sources, tilt and orientation optimization, irradiance on inclined surfaces, shading impact analysis, and site survey basics.

Lecture 5 (2h): PV Market & Economics (Egypt Focus)

Egypt's energy landscape, feed-in tariffs and policies, market opportunities, and basic PV system economics.

Level 2: Grid-Tied PV Systems Engineering

Design and engineer complete grid-connected PV systems

Lecture 6 (2.5h): Inverters & Grid Interaction

Inverter types (string vs central), grid synchronization, fault behavior, and grid code basics.

Lecture 7 (2.5h): BOS Components & Installation

Cables, protection devices, mounting structures, safety practices, and comparison of rooftop vs ground-mount systems.

Lecture 8 (2.5h): System Design Fundamentals

Component selection, system configuration options, cable sizing, protection design, and Performance Ratio (PR).

Lecture 9 (3h): Manual Design Workshop — Grid-Tied

Full system sizing from scratch using manual methods and datasheet-based design, with a complete worked case study.

Lecture 10 (3h): Software Design & Simulation

PV system simulation using industry software, comparison of net metering vs feed-in tariff models, and inverter efficiency analysis.

Lecture 11 (2h): O&M and Troubleshooting

Monitoring systems, fault detection methods, common failure modes, and maintenance strategies.

Level 3: Off-Grid & Hybrid Systems

Design standalone and hybrid PV systems with battery storage

Lecture 12 (2.5h): Off-Grid System Concepts

System configurations, mini-grids and hybrid systems overview, and illustrative case studies.

Lecture 13 (2.5h): Batteries & Load Management

Load estimation, battery technologies (Lead-acid, Li-ion), sizing methodologies, safety considerations, and charge controllers.

Lecture 14 (2.5h): Off-Grid Design Engineering

Full system sizing, voltage drop calculations, protection coordination, wiring, and safety practices.

Lecture 15 (3h): Off-Grid Design Workshop + Practical

Hands-on manual design, software simulation, and an introduction to wiring and commissioning concepts.

Level 4: Solar Pumping Systems

Engineer PV-powered water pumping systems end-to-end

Lecture 16 (2.5h): Pumping Fundamentals

Pump types and curves, irrigation methods, and PV pumping system configurations.

Lecture 17 (3h): Pumping System Design Workshop

Load calculation, pump selection, PV array sizing, complete bill of materials (BOM), and software-assisted design.

Level 5: Projects, Economics & Real-World Engineering

Prepare for real project execution and industry practice

Lecture 18 (2.5h): PV Project Development

Project lifecycle overview, stakeholders, permits and regulations, and risk analysis.

Lecture 19 (2.5h): Financials & Feasibility

Cost trends, ROI and LCOE concepts, financial modeling basics, and a full Egypt-based case study.

Lecture 20 (3h): Final Project + Evaluation

Participants complete a full system design as a capstone project, deliver a presentation, receive instructor feedback, and complete a final assessment.

About the Instructor

Ahmed Metwally is a substation engineering manager with over 16 years of hands-on experience in AIS and GIS substations, with a focus on control and protection schemes. Throughout his career, he has worked closely with multidisciplinary teams across primary, secondary, and telecom systems, gaining practical insight into the challenges of real-world project execution.

He has contributed to cost-saving initiatives that helped optimize designs and vendor specifications, leading to measurable improvements in efficiency. His academic background includes studies in Japanese Corporate Management through Harvard Business School and ongoing MBA studies, reflecting a continuous commitment to learning and development.

Ahmed believes in sharing practical knowledge in a structured, accessible way, and is passionate about helping engineering students bridge the gap between theory and application.
